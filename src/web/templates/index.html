How SISA Works
1. Sharding
Training data is split into S disjoint shards. A separate model is trained per shard.
2. Isolation
Shard models are trained independently — gradients never mix across shards.
3. Slicing
Each shard is further divided into slices with checkpoints after each one.
4. Aggregation
At inference, softmax outputs of all shard models are averaged for the final prediction.

On a forget request, only the affected shard is partially retrained from the last clean checkpoint — achieving up to 4.63× speedup over full retraining (Purchase-100 dataset, Bourtoule et al. 2021).
